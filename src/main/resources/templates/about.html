BMI

BMI or Body Mass Index is a measure of body fat based on height and weight. The way you calculate it is by dividing your body mass(weight) by the square of your height - kg/m"2". The result can be used to determine if your weight might be putting you at risk for health problems.

BMR

Basal Metablic Rate is the total numbers of calories that your body needs to perform basic(basal), life-sustaining functions. Knowing your BMR can help you lose, gain or maintain your weight. We use the Harris-Benedict formula which takes into account gender, age, weight and height. Woman: BMR = 655 + (9.6 × weight in kg) + (1.8 × height in cm) – (4.7 × age in years) Men: BMR = 66 + (13.7 × weight in kg) + (5 × height in cm) – (6.8 × age in years)
